Job Placement (3)

What kind of jobs can I get when I graduate?
That is really up to your needs. Live in NYC? Why not get a high paying job there. Live in Akron Ohio? Maybe remote work would suit you better. What if you want to start your own consulting service? You can do that to. At around session 40 (out of 50) we start you on the job search. You do the leg-work, we provide the inside knowledge on how to nail the job!
What happens if I donâ€™t have a job when I finish the program?
At Devschool, you graduate when you get a job as a coder. That means when you run out of sessions (something that so far hasn't happened) before you get a job, we stick with you. You stay in the school, you still get access to all the sessions and the chat and the support network.
What salaries do your students end up taking?
It all depends on where you live, and where you want to work. Want to be a digital nomad and travel the world while coding for a living? Well, working remote gets you paid less, but you get to travel the world. Do you live in NYC or Silicon Valley? Well, you will make a lot more, but it also costs a lot more. In general we don't collect salary information and it is the wrong metric to focus on anyway. We advise you to take whatever job fits your lifestyle at first. Then put in some dues like you are expected to. Make a good impression and some good business connections, then start looking for more money and a better job. You need your foot in the door first!

Our Curriculum (4)

Should I learn JavaScript or Ruby or a mix?
Ruby is awesome. It can teach you a lot of really cool things about programming. Ruby on Rails makes building websites backed by a relational database fast and easy. However, JavaScript is the future and we will see a slow dying out of Ruby over the next 10 years. Do you want to build apps as an entrepreneur? Then you should do Ruby on Rails and build your application guided by our expert instructors. Do you want to get a job as a programmer building web applications of the future? Well, JavaScript and Node is a much better choice with some Ruby sprinkled in as an elective.
Do I get a detailed curriculum?
There is no detailed curriculum or anything you can read up on in advanced - it is 100% shaped by your personal goals and interests. Our program is based on experience and a real work environment, not school, which makes the idea of a canned list up front much less appealing.
Do you teach any Angular, React, Meteor, or Ruby Rails?
Yes, although we focus on the language and style of programming, data structures, and principles like SOLID more than specific frameworks. If you are taking JavaScript you will learn react, webpack, browserify, gulp, express, and node from us, and then supplement your framework diet with expert instruction from the included license to FrontEndMasters.com. If you are studying Ruby or a mix of the two you will learn Ruby on Rails.
What if I want to take both courses?
You can mix the two courses for the same price, however you still get 50 personal sessions total. You can also pay for both courses and get 100 personal sessions.

How it works (5)

Do I learn from live mentorship or watching videos?
What if I told you, you get to have your cake and eat it too? Well get those forks ready, because you learn from live experts, one on one, coding software together in real time. But you also have full access to egghead.io and frontendmasters.com when you enroll with us. To top it off, those live one on ones with real experts? Those are recorded locally on your machine, automagically, every-time, so you can build your own library of 50+ kick ass screencasts to mark your education milestones!
Does this program include pair programming?
Hell yes! Pair programming is actually quite a bit like the life-blood of our program. You start off by pairing directly with your Instructors, and when you don't know anything yet, they are typing, or telling you what to type. That graduates to you sharing the duties, and eventually when you are towards the end of the program, you pair with other advanced students.
How does the electives system work?
We don't just teach coding, we teach job skills, design skills, management skills, business skills, marketing skills, seo skills, you know. We help you get some skills. Lots of em.
