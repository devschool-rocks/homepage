Some of the awesome stuff you will learn

HTML5

HTML is the heart and soul of the web. It is the foundation on which all the other languages and technologies revolve around and is in constant flux.

CSS3/SASS

Without CSS the web would be a dull and drab place indeed! It is these Cascading Style Sheets that provide all of the color, margin, and fonts to websites.

JavaScript/ES6

JavaScript is the only language embedded in every browser on the planet. It is the mechanism by which content is changed or animated.

Bootstrap

Bootstrap is a framework created by Twitter to provide an easy mechanism to render complex content to a wide variety of screen dimensions.

Express & Node

Node and Express allow us to build interactive real time web applications. Pairing these two skills together will open a lot of doors for you.

Ruby on Rails

Ruby on Rails provides an excellent back-end framework for websites with a lot of data entry going on. It excels at helping devs to quickly build features.

Bash & Zsh

You will learn to use bash and zsh to your advantage. Aliases, dot files, and a common set of configs and tools you can take with you is one of the hallmarks of a programmer. We will help you master the command terminal.

Git

We can use Git to secure our files against loss or corruption, for working on experimental branches, for tracking changes made by other developers, and merging it all back together so it can be deployed live on the internet.

Postgres & SQL

When data must be stored (which is basically all the time), a database is needed to store and retrieve that data At Devschool we primarily focus on tried and true Postgresql and then show you newer formats like Mongo.

Vim

Vim is some super-secret sauce. Not only does it make you look like a wizard while you are creating and editing files, which is a large part of a developer's life, but it also is built to be a very powerful, efficient tool.

OSX

We LOVE Macs here at Devschool. This is not an accident. Not only are they stunningly beautiful machines for doing computer work on, but they are built on UNIX, which is the basis of all those web servers out there.

Gulp

Gulp JS is an amazing web development build tool that can do it all. Compress, minify, uglify. It can even build sitemaps and can be used as a static site generator! (SSG)

The Devschool FAQ

Over the last couple of years we have been compiling questions prospective students just like yourself have asked us. This is our attempt at giving you all the answers you need to make the choice to join Devschool and change your life!

Job Placement (3)

What happens if I don’t have a job when I finish the program?
At Devschool, you graduate when you get a job as a coder. That means when you run out of sessions (something that so far hasn't happened) before you get a job, we stick with you. You stay in the school, you still get access to all the group sessions and the chat and the support network.
What kind of jobs can I get when I graduate?
That is really up to your needs. Live in NYC? Why not get a high paying job there. Live in Akron Ohio? Maybe remote work would suit you better. What if you want to start your own consulting service? You can do that to. At around session 40 (out of 50) we start you on the job search. You do the leg-work, we provide the inside knowledge on how to nail the job!
What salaries do your students end up taking?
It all depends on where you live, and where you want to work. Want to be a digital nomad and travel the world while coding for a living? Well, working remote gets you paid less, but you get to travel the world. Do you live in NYC or Silicon Valley? Well, you will make a lot more, but it also costs a lot more. In general we don't collect salary information and it is the wrong metric to focus on anyway. We advise you to take whatever job fits your lifestyle at first. Then put in some dues like you are expected to. Make a good impression and some good business connections, then start looking for more money and a better job. You need your foot in the door first!

Onboarding (3)

How often do you enroll new classes, how soon can I start?
We onboard every Monday when we have seats available and students to fill those virtual seats. We will never overbook our Instructors (who the fuck wants to work too hard anyway?) and therefore you will get the attention you need. Onboard Monday, start Tuesday. If you need to start the following Friday because you are in Venice... hell yeah! Not a problem.
Can I pay now and enroll to save my seat and start at a later date?
Yeah, we have the ability to do that as well. Sometimes you are ready to commit, but you don't yet have the time to start. We get that, and we have you covered!
How many Units do students usually start with?
1 Unit is 13 hours, most of our students start with 2 Units for 26 hours a week. After you get comfortable with the program and your learning starts to spike, we suggest you move to 3 Units a week for optimal progression. But as always, we are flexible!

Do you take beginners at Devschool or do I need to prep?

Our Pricing (3)

Do you have a way to split the enrollment cost up?
Absolutely! We have a layaway plan, which is 50% down and 50% to start. The first half puts you on our special list of incoming students and you get first pick on the next available seats as soon as you pay the second half of your enrollment. But even before then, you get access to our #slack channel so you can socialize with your fellow Devschoolers and to participate in live group sessions led by programming experts!
Do you offer financing or payments?
We no longer offer this service. We are in the business of getting students to graduate and do something awesome with their lives. Unfortunately when we had a financing option, we had too many dropouts. Once we removed the payment plan option, we stopped losing students.
Do you offer any grants or scholarships?
Yep. We offer the following grants for $500 each: "Minority in tech", "Military service". Can you qualify for more than one? Say you were a female or hispanic veteran? Yep, that would be $1000 off your tuition at Devschool. We don't like war and killing, but we love those who give their all in the service of their fellow humans. We are also highly interested in diversifying this industry.

Our Curriculum (9)

How does the electives system work?
We don't just teach coding, we teach job skills, design skills, management skills, business skills, marketing skills, seo skills, you know. We help you get some skills. Lots of em. You can elect for any session along the way to discuss a large array of topics that will be listed in it's own section.
Should I learn JavaScript or Ruby or a mix?
Ruby is awesome. It can teach you a lot of really cool things about programming. Ruby on Rails makes building websites backed by a relational database fast and easy. However, JavaScript is the future and we will see a slow dying out of Ruby over the next 10 years. Do you want to build apps as an entrepreneur? Then you should do Ruby on Rails and build your application guided by our expert instructors. Do you want to get a job as a programmer building web applications of the future? Well, JavaScript and Node is a much better choice with some Ruby sprinkled in as an elective.
Does this program include pair programming?
Hell yes! Pair programming is actually quite a bit like the life-blood of our program. You start off by pairing directly with your Instructors, and when you don't know anything yet, they are typing, or telling you what to type. That graduates to you sharing the duties, and eventually when you are towards the end of the program, you pair with other advanced students.
What are Units and how do they translate to hours?
A unit is 13 hours of work, and we start everyone with 2 units a week (26 hours) and they can ramp up to 3 units a week (39 hours) or go down to a single unit (13 hours) or even put themselves on hold for awhile if like kicks you in the ass. Which is does from time to time! Don't worry, Devschool is ninja yogamaster flexible!
How often do I meet with my instructor?
After you have your onboarding session, you get your initial 2 Units a week scheduled with your Instructors. Those appointments happen each week unless you need to miss or change one. It is fine to cancel last moment with us. We might be code therapists, but we aren't psychologists! You can just make up that appointment another time.
How long does it take to complete the program?
That depends entirely on you and your own situation. If you do 3 Units a week you could finish in as little as 16 weeks. If you can only handle a single Unit a week, you could be with us for a year. That is ok with us if it is ok with you. We heard you were pretty fun!
Do I learn from live mentorship or watching videos?
What if I told you, you get to have your cake and eat it too? Well get those forks ready, because you learn from live experts, one on one, coding software together in real time. But you also have full access to egghead.io and frontendmasters.com when you enroll with us. To top it off, those live one on ones with real experts? Those are recorded locally on your machine, automagically, every-time, so you can build your own library of 50+ kick ass screencasts to mark your education milestones!
Do you teach any Angular, React, Meteor, or Ruby Rails?
Yes, although we focus on the language and style of programming, data structures, and principles like SOLID more than specific frameworks. If you are taking JavaScript you will learn gulp, express, and node from us, and then suppliment your framework diet with expert instruction from the included licenses to Egghead.io or FrontEndMasters.com. If you are studying Ruby or a mix of the two you will learn Ruby on Rails.
Do I get a detailed curriculum?
There is no detailed curriculum on anything. Our program is based on experience and real work, not school.
